🧭 Method Recommendation Wizard

Answer these questions to get the best meta-analysis method for your data.

Step 1: What type of data do you have?

Dose-Response Data

Multiple exposure levels per study with log relative risks

Binary Outcome Data

2x2 tables (events/total in treatment and control groups)

Continuous Outcome Data

Means and standard deviations for each group

Step 2: How many studies do you have?

Small (< 10 studies)

Consider Hartung-Knapp adjustment for small samples

Medium (10-30 studies)

Standard random-effects methods work well

Large (> 30 studies)

Can use more complex models and subgroup analyses

Step 3: What is the expected heterogeneity?

Low Heterogeneity (I² < 25%)

Fixed-effect model may be appropriate

Moderate Heterogeneity (I² 25-75%)

Random-effects model recommended

High Heterogeneity (I² > 75%)

Investigate sources; consider subgroup analysis
